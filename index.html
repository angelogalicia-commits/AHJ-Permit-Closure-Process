AHJ Permit Closure
After TPI Inspection

Complete process for closing active building permits with the Authority Having Jurisdiction following TPI inspection approval — from verifying the Letter of Compliance to confirming final permit closure.

1
Purpose

This SOP defines the step-by-step process that Terra Energy's Inspections team owns and executes to close active building permits with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) or municipality following successful inspection approval by a Third-Party Inspector (TPI).

This process is entirely owned by Terra Energy — TPI only issues the Letter of Compliance and does not interact with the AHJ on Terra Energy's behalf.

2
Scope

This SOP applies to all Solar PV and Energy Storage System (ESS) projects in Texas that meet all of the following:

· Have undergone TPI inspection and received a Letter of Compliance – Inspection Approval
· Have one or more active building and/or MEP permits pending closure with an AHJ/municipality
· Require formal coordination with the AHJ to finalize and close permits

Step-by-Step Process
1
Receive & verify TPI Letter of Compliance
Details
Download and cross-check every field against project records.

Upon TPI inspection completion, download and review the Letter of Compliance – Inspection Approval. Verify all of the following match your project records exactly:

Project address  ·  Permit numbers (BPIS and MEP)  ·  Contractor name: Terra Energy / NATIONAL SOLAR INSTALLATIONS OF TX LLC  ·  Project type (Solar PV and/or ESS)  ·  Inspector signature and license/accreditation numbers

If any detail does not match, contact TPI immediately for a corrected letter. Do not submit a mismatched letter to the AHJ.
2
Identify the AHJ submission method
Details
Determine whether the AHJ uses a portal or requires direct email.

Method A – AHJ has a portal: Upload the Letter of Compliance as Supplemental Info and all required inspection reports, then send a formal email referencing the portal upload. Email is always required.

Method B – No portal (manual email): Email the AHJ directly with the Letter of Compliance and all applicable inspection reports attached.

If the AHJ contact is unknown: check the permit document, search the municipality's website, or call the building department directly.
3
Determine required inspection reports
Details
Confirm which inspection items apply based on project scope. Example

Review the permit and project scope (Solar PV vs. Solar + ESS) to confirm what's required. Example

E148 Electrical Cover Inspection E156 Electrical Reconnect Inspection E149 Electrical Final B105 Building Final
Not sure which apply? Check the permit documentation or confirm with the AHJ before submitting. Do not guess.
4
Draft & send the permit closure email
Details
Always email the AHJ — even when a portal exists. This creates the official paper trail.

Subject line: Request to Close Permit – [PERMIT NUMBERS] │ [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Include: permit numbers (BPIS + MEP), property address, list of inspections passed, confirmation that documentation is uploaded or attached, and a request to proceed with closure.

Always attach: TPI Letter of Compliance PDF. For Method B, also attach all applicable inspection reports.

Sign with your full name, SME – Inspections title, phone, terraenergy.io, and your Terra Energy email.
5
Log the submission & monitor response
Details
Record submission details and set a follow-up reminder.

Log in the project's Inspections Passed Work Order: note the date sent and AHJ contact used. Set a follow-up reminder.

Standard AHJ review: 24–48 hours (excluding weekends and closures). Follow up if no response within 3 business days.

Special inspections are rare but may be required by certain AHJs. If requested, respond promptly and document the entire exchange.
6
Confirm permit closure & update all records
Details
Monitor status and close out everything once the permit is officially closed.

Monitor via the AHJ portal or wait for a confirmation email. Once confirmed closed:

① Update AHJ permit closure task to Completed
② Notify stakeholders (Interconnection team, project manager)
③ File closure confirmation email/screenshot in the project document folder
④ Upload permit closeout docs to the project
⑤ Mark permit as closed in the Work Order labeled "AHJ Permit Closure"

12
Key Reminders
TPI does not close permits. They only issue the Letter of Compliance. Permit closure is 100% Terra Energy's responsibility.
Always email the AHJ — even if they have a portal. Email provides a formal paper trail and proof of coordination.
Standard AHJ review: 24–48 hours (excluding weekends and closures). Follow up if no response within 3 business days.
Required inspections vary by scope. Always verify against the permit. When unsure, confirm with the AHJ before submitting.
